The Problem

SE pre-call research is a quota tax

Every qualified account requires hours of manual research across SEC filings, news, job boards, procurement portals, federal databases, and campus safety reports. Most SEs skip it or settle for generic AI summaries that could describe any company in the vertical.

Analyze
3 Sonnet subagents (company-bg, tech-and-pain, hiring-signals) process source data in parallel. Each outputs structured JSON with confidence tags.

Opus synthesizer merges all outputs against the persona rule engine. Fires trigger-matched discovery questions. Builds MEDDIC. Renders HTML brief.

What's Different

Not another AI wrapper

Source attribution on every claim

Every factual claim carries [source_url, retrieved_date]. Claims without sources are tagged [INFERENCE] or dropped. Footnote chips link directly to the source.

Anti-genericness engine

Specificity rewrite pass rejects any sentence that could appear unchanged in a brief about a different company. "Insufficient data" beats filler.

Persona-filtered discovery

Discovery questions are generated from trigger templates in the persona file, filled with account-specific data, then filtered by buyer persona (care_about / skip_topics).

MEDDIC qualification overlay

Every brief includes a complete MEDDIC framework with named champions, evidence arrays, confidence tags, and gap analysis per field.

Champion projection

Named individuals scored on 6 weighted factors from public signals. Not a guess — a structured hypothesis with validation questions.

Vendor-agnostic architecture

Swap verkada-se.yml for cisco-se.yml. Triggers, personas, displacement targets, and champion criteria are all config — not code.
